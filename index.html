When you use an AI through an API, you pay for every token it produces. But how do you know how many tokens the AI really used?

What are tokens and why should we care?

The same answer, different prices

Most AI models represent text as using chunks called tokens and charge you based on the token count. However, the same text can be represented in many different ways, called tokenizations. Drag the slider to let change the tokenization of a text generated by an AI and watch what happens to your bill.

Can tokeniations be manipulated ?

3

Pay per character, not per token

If the problem is that tokenizations can be manipulated while users pay by the token count, is there a better way to price AI that leaves nothing to manipulate? There is!:

T We show that:

The only pricing rule that guaranteees there is no incentive for a provider to overcharge is to bill by the character.

The intuition is very simple: the text "Damascus" generated by the AI is eight characters no matter how you many tokens the providers claims that the model used. A character count can't be inflated by changing the tokenization of the text, so there is nothing left to game!

Pay-per-token

The price depends on how the text is encoded, which is a (partially) random process and can be manipulated.

Pay-per-character

The price depends only on the text itself. However you encode it, the count is fixed. There is nothing left to game, removing the economic incentive to overcharge users.
